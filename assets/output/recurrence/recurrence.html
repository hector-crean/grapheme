Recurrence

Definition1:

Recurrence (either depression or mania) is defined as occurring >8 weeks after remission from the acute episode.

Statistics:

  • 90% of patients who have a single manic episode will have future recurrent episodes.
  • Up to 15% of patients will have more than 10 episodes in their lifetime.
  • Risk of recurrence is 3.4X higher if residual symptoms are present vs absent.
  • Time to recurrence is 5X faster if residual symptoms are present vs absent.

Predictors of mania recurrence4:

  • Initial mood-congruent psychosis
  • Lower premorbid occupational status
  • Initial manic presentation
  • Residual manic symptoms

Predictors of depression recurrence4:

  • Higher occupational status
  • Initial mixed presentation
  • Comorbidities
  • Residual depressive symptoms
