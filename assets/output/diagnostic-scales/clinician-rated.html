Diagnostic Scales
Clinician Rated Scales

Thescale measures the severity of illness. It is measured on a scale from 1-5; 1 being normal-not ill at all to 5 being very severely ill.

Thescale measures the severity of drug-induced dyskinesias.

Score Result
0 Normal
4 Severe

Thescale evaluates the severity of akathisia, which is characterized by a feeling of inner restlessness and the urge to move the limbs, especially the legs.
There are three components of the scale:

  • The Objective component assesses on a scale of 0 to 3 the visible behavior of the patient during examination; a rating of 0 signifies normal behavior with only occasional fidgety movement, while 3 indicates that the patient is constantly engaged in restless movements
  • The Subjective component is elicited by direct questioning of the patient and assesses the patient's awareness of, and distress arising from, the akathisia; ratings are from 0 to 3, with 3 indicating greater awareness or distress
  • The Global Clinical Assessment provides an overall evaluation of the severity of akathisia, rated on a scale of 0 to 5; a score of 0 corresponds to normal or absent, while 5 indicates severe akathisia

Themeasures drug-induced parkinsonism symptoms. 10 tasks responses are rated on a scale of 0-4 (normal to severe). A total score of 0-40 is calculated.

Score Result
<3 Normal range
≥6 Indicates a level of disorder for which treatment should be reconsidered
≥12 Requires attention
≥18 Almost certainly requires modification of pharmacotherapy

Themeasures the severity of a depressive episode.

Score Result
0 – 6 Normal
7 – 19 Mild
20 – 34 Moderate
34 + Very Severe Depression

Themeasures Bipolar Spectrum (I, II, cyclothmia), with a threshold for positive diagnosis of > 13.
