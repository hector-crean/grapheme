Residual Symptoms

The presence of residual symptoms in bipolar patients after a major affective episode increases the risk of relapse as well as the severity of future symptoms.

4 separate studies have indicated that recovery in the presence of residual symptoms is a clinically useful marker for early bipolar relapse.

Studies have shown that residual depressive symptoms appear to have an impact on overall functioning and are strongly associated with impairments in interpersonal and occupational functioning, cognitive dysfunction with impairments in professional functioning and stigma with impairments in interpersonal, leisure and cognitive functioning2.
Residual manic symptoms are strongly associated with poor autonomy and financial issues2.

Residual Symptoms and Recurrence

A study showed that both residual manic and depressive symptoms were significantly associated with risk of BD recurrence. In particular, residual manic symptoms were significantly associated with the risk of manic/hypomanic/mixed episode recurrence.

Residual mood symptoms early in recovery appear to be a powerful predictor of recurrence, in particular depression.
