Remission

Definition1

Absence (or near absence) of signs and symptoms of a specified condition (e.g. mania or hypomania) and the lack of a concomitant increase in symptoms of another condition (e.g. depression).

Remission in bipolar depression1

  • Sad mood and ⁄ or loss of interest ⁄ pleasure may not be present, and < 3 of the 7 remaining core
    • criteria may be meaningfully (score > 3 within a range of 1–7) present
  • CGI score ≤ 2

  • HAMD-17 score ≤ 5 or ≤ 7
  • MADRS score ≤ 5 or ≤ 7, or BDRS score ≤ 8
  • Excludes daily functioning

Remission in bipolar mania1

  • Focused on core affective symptoms
  • Focused on the 7 criterion symptom domains identified in DSM to diagnose a manic episode
  • DSM-IV criterion A ≤2; no B criterion rated > 3; no more than two B criteria = 3
  • Initial mixed episodes fulfil recovery criteria; no depression criterion > 3; no more than three criteria = 3
  • CGI-BP ≤2

  • Young Mania Rating Score (YMRS) < 8 or < 5

Factors associated with non-remission2

Female gender
Being unemployed
Poor social function
Non-use of lithium
Use of antidepressants
Younger age
Rapid cycling pattern

Predictors associated with a longer time to reach remission3

  • Psychiatric comorbidities
  • Higher number of previous depressive episodes
  • Higher YMRS score
