Patient Burden

Bipolar disorder greatly diminishes the quality of life of patients compared to patients with other mental disorders.

Depressive symptoms and the number of depressive episodes appear to be primarily responsible for the reduction in quality of life and reduced functioning compared to the manic/hypomanic episodes.

Work Performance

Evidence indicates that the majority of patients with bipolar disorder are not employed and many others are employed only part time. Job-related difficulties are common, and patients with bipolar disorder tend to have higher rates of absenteeism from work compared with working individuals without bipolar disorder.

In the National Institute of Mental Health Systematic Treatment Enhancement Program for Bipolar Disorders, among patients who entered in a depressive episode, annual income was low, with 91% having earned less than $30 000 in the previous year.

Results from the national depressive and manic-depressive association showed that ~60% of patients with BD were unemployed, even amongst those with higher education. Furthermore 88% of the responders reported occupational difficulties.

Health

Chronic health conditions:

Patients with BD have a higher occurrence of chronic health conditions such as cardiovascular disease and type 2 diabetes in comparison to the general population.

Cardiovascular mortality amongst BD patients is almost 2x that seen in the general population which may be linked to the higher rates of obesity seen amongst BD patients.

The mechanisms behind why BD patients have a higher prevalence for chronic health conditions is largely unclear. However, it has been hypothesised that it is due to factors such as a sedentary lifestyle, smoking, diet and unrecognised risk factors such as inflammation etc.

Life expectancy and suicide rates6

  • Life expectancy for patients with severe mental illness (SMI) such as bipolar disorder is reduced by 9-17 years in comparison to the general population.

Relationships

Patients with BD have higher divorce rates.

BD patients who were married, living as married, divorced, or separated had worse functional impairment compared with patients who were single or never married.

BD patients have lower fertility rates in comparison to those without the illness.

Social functioning9

Patients with BD have smaller social circles and fewer social interactions when compared to the general population.

Patients with BD are less likely to reach social milestones such as marriage or equivalent relationships when compared to the general population.

Caregiver Burden

The burden carers experience when caring for a family member with a psychiatric illness has been classified into two categories:

  1. – what happens to family life that can be observed and verified
  2. – personal feelings of the caregiver related to the burden

The majority of caregivers have been shown to be family members – 37% parents, 32% partner and 24% another relative.

Recent studies have shown that caregivers burden may influence the BD patients outcome, those that are worse effected impact negatively on the patients medication adherence and on the risk of future mood episodes.

Caregivers suffer more in the depressive episodes compared to the manic/hypomanic episode.

Most commonly caregivers were disturbed by aggressive and violent behavior (17%), suicidal ideas/acts (10%), overactivity, overtalkativeness and impulse spends (each 4%).

Health1

Caregivers of patients with BD report higher levels of stress, poorer general health, increased visits to their primary care physicians and higher numbers of physiological and psychological conditions such as depression.

44% of caregivers had experienced violence from the BD patient.

Violence was more common towards caregivers that were partners.

Relationships1

Separations/divorce due to illness related problems are common.

81% of caregivers were distressed by their relationship with the patient when they were acutely unwell, 64% of these caregivers described the stress as severe. However when in remission, 80% of caregivers described their relationship with the BD patient as close, with 49% stating the illness had bought them closer together.

92% of caregivers which were partners found it difficult to maintain their relationship.

Social functioning1

Caregivers experience serious disruption in social and leisure activities, especially partner caregivers.

Loss of social functioning for caregivers can negatively impact the clinical outcome for the BD patient.
