Switch

Switch in Bipolar Disorder

  • Switch is the occurrence of an episode of a different illness before recovery, such as a manic episode after depression, or depression after mania.
  • Depressive switch occurs from immediately after the manic episode to up to 4 weeks into remission.
  • Manic switch occurs from immediately after the depressive episode to up to 8 weeks into remission.
  • Approximately 19-29% of bipolar disorder patients experience switch.
  • Switching can be due to stress, lifestyle factors and treatment such as antidepressants or ECT.
