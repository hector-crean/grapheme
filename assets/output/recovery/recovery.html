Recovery

Definition of Recovery

As the majority of bipolar patients remain vulnerable to subsequent episodes indefinitely, the definition of recovery refers to recovery from the last mood episode, not recovery from the illness itself. Therefore recovery can be lost due to recurrence.

ISBD Task Force Definition:

A minimum period of 8 consecutive weeks of absence or near absence of depressive and manic symptoms.

Patient Definition:

Patient advocacy groups often focus on the subjective (personal) standards of recovery, including social and occupational functioning.

Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMSHA) defines recovery as: 'a process of change through which individuals improve their health and wellness, live a self-directed life, and strive to reach their full potentialâ€™.

There are four major dimensions supporting a life in recovery:

  • Health: overcoming or managing one's disease or symptoms and making choices that support physical and emotional well-being.
  • Home: having a stable and safe place to live.
  • Purpose: conducting meaningful daily activities and being able to participate in society.
  • Community: relationships and social networks providing support.

Recovery Dimensions3

Syndromal

No longer meeting DSM criteria for an acute mood episode.

Symptomatic

Low scores on standard ratings of mania and depression that indicate near-absence of symptoms.

Functional

Regaining individual premorbid levels of psychosocial, residential, and occupational status.

Likelihood of Syndromal, Symptomatic and Functional Recovery4:

In bipolar patients followed two years after an initial manic/mixed episode;

  • 97.6% achieved syndromal recovery
  • 71.7% achieved symptomatic recovery
  • 43.1% achieved functional recovery

Predictors of Syndromal Recovery4:

  • Older age
  • Lower initial depression rating
  • Shorter hospitalization stay for index episode.
  • Being married

    • Predictors of Functional Recovery:

  • Higher level of education.
  • Being married.
  • Older age.
  • Shorter hospitalization stay for index episode.
  • Caucasian race.
